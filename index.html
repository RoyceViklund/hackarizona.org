Flying In

We highly recommend that out-of-state attendees reserve their plane tickets as soon as possible to minimize cost. If possible, you should fly into Tucson International Airport, as opposed to Phoenix Sky Harbor.

Tucson International -> University of Arizona - Take Bus #25 from the airport to the Ronstadt Transit Center then transfer to either the Modern Street Car, or Bus #6 to the University Boulevard stop.
Routes and Times Here.

Phoenix Sky Harbor -> University of Arizona - See the options listed under in-state travel.

Driving In

There are a number of shuttle services from most major cities in Arizona that will take you straight to the University of Arizona campus. Links to the most reliable of these services are listed below.

Option 1 - arizonashuttle.com - This service will cost roughly 40 dollars one-way, however it is clean and offers a broad range of travel times. When choosing your drop-off point, select University.

Option 2 - greyhound.com - A more economic option, the Greyhound bus offers shuttle services from most cities in Arizona to downtown Tucson. The Greyhound will drop you off at the centrally-located Ronstadt Center. From the Ronstadt center, take the Suntran Streetcar to the University. The cost for a one-day Suntran pass is ~ $4.00.

Judging


Submit your hack on Sunday, 1/15 by 9AM on HackerEarth.


The Project Exposition will take place from 10:00am to 1:00pm Sunday morning inside the Student Union's Grand Ballroom. Sponsors, mentors, judges, and members of the public will be granted entry to view projects at 10:00am. Winners of Hack Arizona categories and sponsor prizes will be announced online at 9:00am the following morning, Monday, January 16th.

Eligibility

  1. 1) Granted entry via wristband
  2. 2) All attendees must sign up for a HackerEarth account and setup a team for Hack Arizona 2017
  3. 3) Submit their project on HackerEarth by 8am.
  4. 4) Report to Grand Ballroom for placement by 9am
  5. 5) Demo their project during Project Exposition.

Judging Criteria

Originality: Has your project been done before? Is there novelty present?
Creativity: Does your project have a non-obvious key element? Is there artistic merit present?
Difficulty: How hard was your project to pull off during the event? Did you pull it off?
Value Proposition: Does the project demonstrate a clear utility and is the team able to communicate that?
